Basic Income and Expenses of a Company

  • Operating Expenses (OPEX): Expenses needed to operate/maintain the business (sometimes synonymous with SG&A). In the broader sense, we can think of it as: COGS + SG&A + R&D + D&A
  • Cost of Goods Sold (COGS): The direct costs associated with producing goods (e.g. direct materials, direct labor, equipment costs, overhead costs for facility).
  • Selling, General & Administrative Expenses (SG&A): Typically costs associated with a company's overall overhead. It can be thought of as all non-COGS operational expenses. (e.g. accounting, legal, corporate office overhead, advertising, marketing, rent)
  • Research & Development (R&D): Reseach & Development (which are tax-deductible)
  • Depreciation & Amortization (D&A): The depreciation of CAPEX assets are accounted for in here.
  • Capital Expenditures (CAPEX): Purchases that a business makes as an investment (e.g. real estate, factory, computers, furniture, intellectual property, copyrights, patents) to help it generate more revenue. CAPEX purchases are not immediately reported on an income statement. Rather, it is treated as an asset on the balance sheet, that is deducted over the course of several years as a depreciation expense in D&A. Ideally, the increase in revenue made from this CAPEX investment would outweigh the depreciation rate of the investment.
  • Revenue: Total Worth of a company's goods & services sold
  • Gross Profit: Revenue - COGS
  • EBITDA: Gross Profit - SG&A - R&D
  • Operating Income/EBIT: Revenue - OPEX
  • Net Income: Operating Income - Taxes - Interest Expenses on Debts

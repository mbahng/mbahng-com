Definition 1.1 (Vector Space)
A vector space $V$ over a field $\mathbb{F}$ (usually $\mathbb{R}$ or $\mathbb{C}$) is a set that is algebraically closed under the operations:
  • $+: V \times V \longrightarrow V$
  • $\times: \mathbb{F} \times V \longrightarrow V$
$V$ is also an additive abelian group, with additional axioms as follows: given $\lambda, \mu \in \mathbb{F}$ and $v, u \in V$,
  • $(\lambda + \mu) v = \lambda v + \mu v$
  • $\lambda (v + u) = \lambda v + \lambda u$
  • $(\lambda \mu) v = \lambda (\mu v) = \mu (\lambda v)$
A vector is simply an element of a vector space.
